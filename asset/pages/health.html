Understanding Mental Health

Mental health is essential to overall health and wellbeing. Learn about common conditions, warning signs, and how mental health affects everyone differently.

Explore Conditions Warning Signs

What is Mental Health?

Mental health includes our emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how we think, feel, and act. It also helps determine how we handle stress, relate to others, and make choices.

Mental health is important at every stage of life, from childhood and adolescence through adulthood. Mental health exists on a continuum, and everyone experiences it differently.

Understanding Common Mental Health Conditions

Learn about the most common mental health conditions and their impact

Anxiety Disorders

Excessive fear, worry, and related behavioral disturbances. Symptoms include panic attacks, social anxiety, and specific phobias.

Common Symptoms:

  • Excessive worrying
  • Restlessness or feeling on edge
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Physical symptoms like rapid heartbeat
Learn more about anxiety

Depression

Persistent feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and loss of interest in activities. It affects how you feel, think, and handle daily activities.

Common Symptoms:

  • Persistent sad or empty mood
  • Loss of interest in activities
  • Changes in appetite or weight
  • Difficulty sleeping or oversleeping
Learn more about depression

Bipolar Disorder

Characterized by extreme mood swings that include emotional highs (mania or hypomania) and lows (depression).

Common Symptoms:

  • Periods of elevated mood and energy
  • Depressive episodes with low energy
  • Irregular sleep patterns
  • Impulsive behavior during manic episodes
Learn more about bipolar disorder

PTSD

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder develops after experiencing or witnessing a traumatic event. It involves flashbacks, anxiety, and intrusive thoughts.

Common Symptoms:

  • Flashbacks or nightmares
  • Avoidance of trauma reminders
  • Negative changes in mood
  • Hypervigilance and startle response
Learn more about PTSD

OCD

Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder involves unwanted, intrusive thoughts (obsessions) and repetitive behaviors (compulsions).

Common Symptoms:

  • Unwanted intrusive thoughts
  • Repetitive behaviors or rituals
  • Excessive cleaning or checking
  • Need for symmetry and order
Learn more about OCD

Eating Disorders

Serious conditions related to persistent eating behaviors that negatively impact health, emotions, and ability to function.

Common Symptoms:

  • Extreme weight concerns
  • Distorted body image
  • Restrictive eating patterns
  • Binge eating or purging behaviors
Learn more about eating disorders

Warning Signs to Watch For

Recognizing these signs can help you or someone you care about get help early

Emotional Changes

  • Persistent sadness or irritability
  • Feeling hopeless or helpless
  • Excessive fear, worry, or anxiety
  • Mood swings that affect relationships

Behavioral Changes

  • Withdrawal from social activities
  • Changes in sleep or appetite
  • Increased substance use
  • Difficulty performing daily tasks

Cognitive Changes

  • Difficulty concentrating or making decisions
  • Memory problems
  • Racing thoughts or confusion
  • Intrusive or unwanted thoughts

Physical Symptoms

  • Frequent headaches or stomach issues
  • Unexplained aches and pains
  • Extreme fatigue or low energy
  • Changes in appetite or weight

Myths vs. Facts About Mental Health

Separating misconceptions from reality

MYTH

Mental health problems don't affect children or teenagers.

FACT

50% of mental health conditions begin by age 14, and 75% begin by age 24.

MYTH

People with mental health conditions are violent and unpredictable.

FACT

The vast majority of people with mental illness are no more violent than anyone else.

MYTH

You can't prevent mental health problems.

FACT

Many mental health conditions can be prevented or their impact reduced through early intervention.

MYTH

Mental health problems are a sign of weakness.

FACT

Mental health conditions are medical conditions, not character flaws or signs of weakness.
